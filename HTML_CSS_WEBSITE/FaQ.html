FAQ

Wild animals have impressive natural abilities, such as foraging and hunting for food, self-defense and protection of themselves and their offspring, the ability to form social bonds within families and groups, and navigation in the natural environment using their senses of sight, smell, and hearing.

Ecological disturbances include food source depletion, habitat destruction, environmental pollution, deforestation, and disruption of the natural life cycle of wild animals. These disturbances make the environment less stable and dangerous for their survival.

Humans can influence the lives of wild animals by conserving natural habitats, ensuring safe transportation systems, minimizing human impact on wild areas, and providing legal protection and rehabilitation for endangered species.

Examples of endangered wild animals include pandas, tigers, koalas, eagles, and sea turtles. The reasons for their endangerment include habitat loss, human resource consumption, hunting, and direct threats from predators.

Competition among wild animals is the natural result of limited resources such as food, water, territory, and other critical points in the environment. This competition can influence feeding behavior, scent marking, and joining groups or families for protection.
